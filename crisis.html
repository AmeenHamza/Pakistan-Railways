PR adversely fails to follow schedule time causing huge loss to national exchequer

Pakistan Railway trains have adversely failed to follow the schedule time causing millions of rupees loss to national exchequer. The punctuality of trains has reduced from 59 percent to 42 percent.

According to details, during the financial year 2022-23, Pakistan Railway trains were delayed by 18596 hours due to which thousands of litres additional fuel was consume.

Official documents said that in 2022-23, Mail and Express trains schedule maintenance was reduced by a total 17 percent.

The schedule maintenance of inter-city trains remained at 97 percent in 2021-22, in 2022-23 schedule maintenance of intercity trains was recorded at 85 percent while overall of the schedule maintenance during 2021-22 remained 82 percent. Over schedule maintenance of trains in 2022-23 was recorded as 79 percent.

The documents further stated that engineering restriction caused 4557 hours in against the scheduled time while was 916 more than 2022-23. On the other hand, accidents caused 720 hours delay, bad weather caused 6029 hours delay which was 4163 hour more than previous financial year.

Govt taking steps to rehabilitate Pakistan Railways: PM

Prime Minister Muhammad Shehbaz Sharif has directed the authorities concerned to work on upgradation on Railway Main Line One (ML-1) on priority basis.

  • Presiding over a review meeting regarding matters relating to Pakistan Railways in Islamabad today, he said ML-1 is the backbone of Pakistan Railways.
  • The Prime Minister said Pakistan will also provide trade corridors to other regional countries in future through its railway system and ports. He said the government is taking various steps on priority basis for the rehabilitation of Pakistan Railways.
  • The Prime Minister said people are utilizing and taking benefits from education, health and communication projects including motorways initiated during the PML-N term.
  • He also lauded the efforts of the officials of Pakistan Railways for restoration of rail network after the recent devastating flood in the country.
  • The meeting was informed that Railways ticketing system has been completely digitized while work on digitalization of other sections of Railways is underway.

Rahim Yar Khan Accident

October 31, 2019: More than 70 people died in a fiery explosion on a moving train, which officials later blamed on a short-circuit fire. The incident occurred near Rahim Yar Khan, Punjab.

Two trains collided at Karachi’s Landhi railway station, killing 22 people in Nov, 2016

Karachi Accident

November 3, 2016: Two trains collided at Karachi’s Landhi railway station, killing 22 people. The accident occurred in Gaddafi Town Landhi when Zakaria Express collided with the stationary Fareed Express.

Train crash near Multan left six dead and more than 150 wounded in 2016

Multan Accident

September 15, 2016: At least six people were killed and over 150 injured when Karachi-bound Awam Express collided with a freight train near Multan.
